bio

HANS BILGER is from Brooklyn, New York. The eldest in a musical household, he spent his childhood playing folk and country gospel in a family band. The experience informed his later studies of jazz and experimental music. His mentors include the bassists Greg Cohen (Ornette Coleman, Laurie Anderson, Tom Waits), Paul Kowert (Punch Brothers), Axel Scherka (Staatskapelle Berlin), J.A. Granelli (Mose Allison), and others.

After completing his B.S. at Yale University in 2016, Bilger moved to Austin, Texas, where he became a sought-after sideman and session bassist. His debut solo album, Meridian, was released in 2019 by Good Child Music. Bilger has been composer-in-residence for Brooklyn-based dance company Cora Dance since 2017.

He is also fascinated by the natural world. A current PhD candidate in biology at the University of Texas at Austin, he has published scientific papers on musical structure in birdsong and the anatomy of the avian voice box. Bilger is now based in Berlin, Germany, where he splits his time between performing, recording, and studying the evolution of animal voices at the Museum für Naturkunde.
